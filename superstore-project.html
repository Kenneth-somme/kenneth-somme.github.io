Superstore Overview Report

Sales, Profit & Discount Analysis (2014–2017)

Excel

Project Overview

This project explores the sales and profit performance of a retail superstore from 2014 to 2017. I analyzed transaction-level data to identify which regions and product categories were underperforming — and investigated the role of discounting as a driver of profit loss.

The project involved cleaning the dataset in Excel before using pivot tables and charts to extract trends, presenting the findings through an interactive Excel dashboard.

Objective

To identify the key areas where the store was losing profit or leaving money on the table — examining regional and category-level performance to find the root causes behind underdelivering results.

Features

Sales Trend 2014–2017: Year-over-year tracking of total revenue, revealing steady growth with consistent Q4 seasonality spikes.

Category Sales vs. Profit: Profit margin comparison across product categories, exposing Furniture's critically low 2.49% margin despite high sales volume.

Regional Sales vs. Profit: Side-by-side comparison of sales and profit across all four regions, highlighting the stark gap between West and Central.

Discount Impact Analysis: Examination of how discount levels affect profit per order — and how frequently damaging discounts above 20% are being applied.

Interactive Dashboard: All findings visualized in Excel with filters, trend lines, and drill-down capabilities for deeper exploration.

Results

The data confirmed steady revenue growth from $484k in 2014 to $733k in 2017. However, a deeper look revealed a significant profitability problem driven by aggressive discounting. Of 9,994 total orders, 1,393 (13.9%) used discounts above 20% — a threshold that consistently results in negative profit per order.

This pattern is most damaging in the Central region, which generated $501k in sales but only $39k in profit. Central applies aggressive discounts on 27.7% of orders, compared to just 3.7% in the West — the top-performing region with $108k in profit. Furniture tells a similar story: a 25.6% rate of heavy discounting across its orders explains why the category achieves only a 2.49% profit margin despite being a high-revenue product line.

Recommendation: The data points to discounting discipline as the highest-leverage fix available. Implementing clearer discount guidelines — particularly for Furniture sales and Central region operations — could significantly improve margins without requiring any change to pricing or product strategy. For Furniture specifically, the heavy reliance on discounts to close sales suggests a training opportunity: equipping sales staff with better product knowledge and value-selling techniques could reduce the need for aggressive discounting and directly improve the category's profit margin.
